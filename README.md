
# SQL Exploratory Data Analysis Project

## 📌 Project Overview
This project demonstrates Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) using SQL to uncover business insights from sales data. The analysis focuses on identifying customer behavior, product performance, revenue trends, and overall business patterns through advanced SQL queries.

The goal of this project is to showcase practical SQL skills used by Data Analysts to transform raw data into actionable insights.

---

## 🎯 Business Objectives

This analysis aims to answer key business questions:

- Which products generate the highest revenue?
- Who are the most valuable customers?
- How have sales changed over time?
- Which product categories perform best?
- What are the purchasing patterns across different customer segments?
- Which regions contribute the most to sales?

---

## 🛠️ Skills Demonstrated

- SQL Query Writing
- Data Exploration
- Aggregations & Grouping

## 📊 Analysis Performed
### 1. Database Initialization
- Created and configured the database environment required for analysis.
- Prepared the dataset for exploratory analysis.

### 2. Database Exploration
- Explored database structure, schemas, and tables.
- Examined available dimensions and measures.

### 3. Dimensions Exploration
- Analyzed dimensional attributes such as customers, products, and categories.
- Identified unique values and business entities.

### 4. Date Range Exploration
- Determined the historical coverage of the dataset.
- Identified the earliest and latest transaction dates.

### 5. Measures Exploration
- Analyzed key business metrics including sales, orders, quantity sold, and revenue.
- Generated overall business performance summaries.

### 6. Magnitude Analysis
- Evaluated the scale and contribution of products, categories, and customers.
- Measured revenue and sales distribution across business dimensions.

### 7. Ranking Analysis
- Ranked products, customers, and categories based on performance metrics.
- Identified top and bottom performers.

### 8. Change Over Time Analysis
- Analyzed trends in sales and revenue over time.
- Evaluated monthly and yearly business growth patterns.

### 9. Cumulative Analysis
- Calculated running totals and cumulative business metrics.
- Tracked growth progression over time.

### 10. Performance Analysis
- Compared actual performance across different business entities.
- Evaluated key performance indicators (KPIs).

### 11. Data Segmentation Analysis
- Segmented customers and products into meaningful groups.
- Identified high-value and low-value segments.

### 12. Part-to-Whole Analysis
- Measured contribution percentages of products, categories, and customers.
- Determined how individual segments contribute to total business performance.

### 13. Customer Report Analysis
- Generated customer-focused reports and insights.
- Analyzed customer purchasing behavior and value.

### 14. Product Report Analysis
- Generated product performance reports.
- Evaluated product sales, revenue contribution, and rankings.
